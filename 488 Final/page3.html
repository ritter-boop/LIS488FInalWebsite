Simmons University

I am currently studying at Simmons University for my MLIS with a concentration in Cultural Heritage Informatics. According to the Kent State School of Information:

"Cultural heritage informatics (CHI) is an emerging field of interdisciplinary research and practice concerned with the role of information and computing technologies (ICTs)
to support the creation, capture, organization, and pluralization of culture, in whatever form, as heritage."

To me, CHI means increasing access to culture as a final product, ie museums, libraries, theaters, etc., while also increasing access to the means necessary for contributing
to that culture. I want everyone to see themselves represented, to feel like they have something to contribute, and to feel like public institutions are indeed made for them.

At Simmons, some of my selected coursework includes:
-Digital Stewardship and Digital Preservation sources
-Learning technology used by information professionals such as LMSs, SpringShare, LibGuides, HTML, etc.
-Exploring the ethics behind reference services, information seeking behaviors
